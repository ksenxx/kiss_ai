# Related Work

Our work sits at the intersection of three lines of research: (i) time-series forecasting for financial applications, (ii) end-to-end / decision-focused learning that couples prediction with a downstream optimization objective, and (iii) uncertainty quantification, in particular conformal calibration, for downstream risk control. We survey these areas below and situate the proposed Risk-aware Time-Series Predict-and-Allocate (RTS-PnO) framework within them.

**Time-series forecasting.** Modern deep forecasters underpin virtually all data-driven fund-allocation pipelines. Probabilistic recurrent models such as DeepAR [1](https://arxiv.org/abs/1704.04110) and interpretable basis-expansion architectures such as N-BEATS [2](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.10437) established strong baselines for multi-horizon forecasting. A second wave of Transformer-based models pushed long-horizon accuracy further: Informer introduced ProbSparse attention for long sequences [3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.07436), Autoformer combined series decomposition with auto-correlation [4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.13008), and FEDformer exploited frequency-domain structure [5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.12740). More recent designs continue to advance the state of the art, including patch-based tokenisation in PatchTST [6](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.14730), variate-wise attention in iTransformer [7](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.06625), and 2D temporal-variation modelling in TimesNet [8](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.02186). A parallel line of work has questioned whether Transformer complexity is necessary at all, showing that simple linear baselines can match or surpass these models on standard benchmarks [9](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.13504). All of these forecasters remain purely prediction-oriented, and none of them account for how their forecasts will be consumed by a downstream allocation decision — a gap our framework explicitly addresses in a forecasting-model-agnostic manner.

**Predict-then-Optimize and decision-focused learning.** The naive pipeline of training a forecaster with a statistical loss and then plugging its point predictions into an optimizer is well known to induce a "goal mismatch" between the learning objective and the decision objective. The Smart "Predict, then Optimize" (SPO) framework [10](https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.08005) formalised this gap by defining the SPO loss directly on decision regret and deriving a convex SPO+ surrogate. In parallel, task-based end-to-end learning showed that differentiating through a stochastic program can yield forecasters better aligned with the downstream task, including in energy scheduling and inventory problems [11](https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.04529). OptNet made quadratic-program layers differentiable and thus embeddable within neural pipelines [12](https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.00443), and subsequent work extended decision-focused learning to combinatorial problems on graphs [13](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.13732). A recent survey consolidates foundations, benchmarks, and open challenges of decision-focused learning [14](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.13565). Portfolio-style allocation has also been tackled with deep reinforcement learning, e.g. the cryptocurrency portfolio manager of Jiang et al. [15](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.10059); such approaches learn allocations directly but typically discard explicit forecasts and struggle to expose calibrated uncertainty. RTS-PnO instead retains a modular forecaster while performing end-to-end objective-aligned training around it, so any of the forecasters above can be plugged in without bespoke redesign.

**Uncertainty quantification and conformal calibration.** Because SOTA forecasters introduce non-trivial predictive uncertainty that can dominate allocation regret, calibrating this uncertainty is essential for risk-aware allocation. Conformal prediction offers distribution-free finite-sample coverage guarantees, and a comprehensive introduction is given by Angelopoulos and Bates [16](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.07511). Extensions to sequential and non-exchangeable data are particularly relevant to financial forecasting: EnbPI provides ensemble-based conformal intervals tailored to time series [17](https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.09107), and Adaptive Conformal Inference dynamically adjusts miscoverage under distribution shift [18](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.00170). RTS-PnO builds on this line by adaptively calibrating forecasting uncertainty and injecting it into the allocation objective, thereby unifying decision-focused training with distribution-free risk control and yielding consistent gains over predict-only, Predict-then-Optimize, and RL baselines across Currency, Stock, and Crypto benchmarks as well as a live Cross-Border Payment deployment.
