# Related Work

## Ranking Models for Recommendation and Advertising

Modern large-scale recommender and online advertising systems rely on deep ranking models to score huge candidate pools according to user-item relevance, typically formulated as click-through rate (CTR) or conversion rate (CVR) prediction. Foundational architectures such as Neural Collaborative Filtering [1](https://arxiv.org/abs/1708.05031) and DeepFM [2](https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.04247) demonstrated that neural networks can effectively model high-order feature interactions between users and items. Subsequent industrial designs, including the Entire-Space Multi-Task Model (ESMM) for post-click conversion estimation [3](https://arxiv.org/abs/1804.07931), the Search-based Interest Model (SIM) for lifelong sequential behavior [4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.05639), and the TWo-stage Interest Network (TWIN) deployed at Kuaishou [5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.02352), push ranking accuracy further by leveraging richer user interaction signals. While these models excel at ordering candidates, their raw predicted scores are typically trained with proxy losses such as pointwise cross-entropy or pairwise ranking objectives and therefore do not necessarily reflect the true absolute click or watch probability required by downstream tasks such as bidding, ad auctions, or watch-time estimation.

## Post-hoc Calibration for Prediction Scores

The mismatch between predicted and empirical event rates has motivated a rich line of research on prediction calibration. Classical post-hoc calibrators fit an order-preserving mapping between raw scores and observed frequencies. Guo et al. showed that even highly accurate deep networks tend to be miscalibrated, and that temperature scaling can effectively rectify their probabilities [6](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.04599). In the context of CTR prediction, Field-aware Calibration extends isotonic-style binning to be conditional on side features, yielding stronger reliability in industrial deployments [7](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.10713). Doubly-Adaptive Calibration further refines this direction by matching posterior probabilities with an adaptive parametric family suited to skewed advertising data [8](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.07295). More recent efforts push expressiveness by ensembling piecewise-linear shape functions across fields [9](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.09507) and by injecting confidence signals to reweight bin-level statistics [10](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.17655). A comprehensive survey by Kweon reviews the broader landscape of confidence calibration for recommender systems and highlights the recurring tension between calibration granularity and monotonicity constraints [11](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.16325).

## Joint Optimization of Ranking and Calibration

A parallel line of work argues that calibration should not be treated purely post-hoc and instead should be jointly optimized with the ranking objective. The Joint Ranking–Calibration hybrid model of Sheng et al. contextualizes the calibrator per-request while sharing representations with the base ranker [12](https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.06164). Regression-Compatible Listwise Objectives develop loss functions that preserve calibration under listwise learning-to-rank formulations [13](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.01494). Lin et al. analyze how commonly-used ranking losses degrade calibration under sparse user feedback and propose remedies to reconcile the two goals [14](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.14144). The Self-boosted Framework for Calibrated Ranking couples an inner ranker with a distilled calibration head that mutually reinforce each other during training [15](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.08010). Calibration-compatible listwise distillation further transfers privileged-feature knowledge without compromising probability calibration [16](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.08727). These approaches share the assumption that the calibrator adheres to a predefined, often piecewise-linear or logistic, order-preserving form, which limits expressiveness in complex industrial scenarios and motivates a more flexible parameterization such as the one proposed in our work.

## Monotonic Neural Networks and Normalizing Flows

Because the calibrator must be strictly monotonic to preserve the ranking order, our approach draws on advances in expressive monotone function approximators. Wehenkel and Louppe introduced Unconstrained Monotonic Neural Networks (UMNN), which parameterize an arbitrary monotonic mapping as the integral of a strictly positive free-form network, avoiding the restrictive weight constraints of earlier monotone architectures [17](https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.05164). UMNNs are closely related to autoregressive normalizing flows, whose theoretical foundations and modern variants have been thoroughly reviewed by Papamakarios et al. [18](https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.02762) and by Kobyzev et al. [19](https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.09257). Compared with piecewise-linear or beta-family calibrators employed in the aforementioned recommendation systems, UMNN-based calibrators offer universal approximation of monotonic transformations while retaining differentiability, making them an ideal backbone for a highly expressive calibrator. Our work builds on these ingredients and complements them with a Smooth Calibration Loss that enforces a necessary condition for perfect calibration, thereby providing an effective learning signal for the highly flexible monotonic network in large-scale video ranking systems.
