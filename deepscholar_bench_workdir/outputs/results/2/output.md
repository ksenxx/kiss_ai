# Related Work

## Co-Speech Gesture Generation

A long line of research has studied how to synthesize body language from speech. Early data-driven methods learned speaker-specific mappings from audio to 2D pose using adversarial training [1](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.04160), and later extended to 3D conversational gestures learned directly from in-the-wild video [2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.06837). Multi-modal context, including text, audio and speaker identity, was subsequently shown to improve naturalness and semantic alignment [3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.02119), while hierarchical audio-to-gesture models further captured cross-modal associations at multiple temporal scales [4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.13161). Rhythm-aware neural embeddings introduced explicit beat structure into the mapping between speech and motion [5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.01448), and denoising diffusion models rapidly became a dominant paradigm for producing diverse, high-quality gestures conditioned on audio [6](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.09119) or on CLIP-based style prompts [7](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.14613). The BEAT corpus enabled large-scale semantic and emotional evaluation for such systems [8](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.05297), and more recent works pursue **holistic** synthesis of face, hands and body: EMAGE unifies expressive masked audio-gesture modeling [9](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.00374), DiffSHEG jointly generates 3D expression and gesture in real time [10](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.04747), and ProbTalk focuses on coordinated, variable whole-body motion [11](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.00368). Our work follows this trajectory toward holistic nonverbal generation, but frames the problem inside a language-model interface rather than as an audio-to-motion regressor.

## Speech-Driven Facial Animation

Nonverbal cues from the face have been extensively studied under the umbrella of talking-face and 3D facial-animation synthesis. VOCA learned subject-agnostic 3D speaking styles from 4D scans [12](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.03079), MeshTalk disentangled cross-modal audio-face factors for expressive mesh animation [13](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08223), and FaceFormer introduced autoregressive transformers to model long-range dependencies in speech-driven animation [14](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.05329). CodeTalker demonstrated that a **discrete motion prior** learned via vector quantization sharply improves lip-sync realism and codebook-based generation [15](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.02379) — a design choice that directly inspires the VQ-based nonverbal tokens used in our framework. Emotion-controllable variants such as EmoTalk [16](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.11089) and EMOTE [17](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.08990) further disentangle content from affect, while image-space approaches like Wav2Lip [18](https://arxiv.org/abs/2008.10010) and SadTalker [19](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.12194) produce photo-realistic talking heads from single images. These systems, however, treat text/speech as a one-way conditioning signal and do not model nonverbal cues as part of a bidirectional conversational token stream.

## Multimodal and Motion Language Models

Large language models have been rapidly extended beyond text. LLaMA established strong open foundation models for language [20](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.13971), which were then adapted to vision (LLaVA) [21](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.08485) and to video plus audio understanding (Video-LLaMA) [22](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.02858). In the audio domain, AudioLM cast waveform generation as language modeling over discrete tokens [23](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.03143), VALL-E showed that neural-codec LMs enable zero-shot text-to-speech [24](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.02111), and SpeechGPT [25](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.11000) and AudioPaLM [26](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.12925) unified speech understanding and generation with textual reasoning in a single decoder. A parallel line treats human motion itself as a "foreign language": T2M-GPT tokenizes motion with a VQ-VAE and generates it autoregressively from text [27](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.06052), MotionGPT further couples motion tokens with an instruction-tuned LM for multi-task control [28](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.14795), and AvatarGPT extends this idea to planning and long-horizon motion synthesis [29](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.16468). MARS builds on this "modality-as-tokens" recipe, but is, to our knowledge, the first to jointly tokenize **text, facial expression and body language** for conversational generation, enabled by the large-scale, time-aligned annotations in the VENUS dataset.
