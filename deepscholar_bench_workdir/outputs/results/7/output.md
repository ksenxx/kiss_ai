# Related Work

**Linear representations and probing of concepts.** A long line of work has shown that neural networks encode high-level concepts as approximately linear directions in their internal activation space. Early probing methods trained linear classifiers on intermediate features to expose the information carried by each layer [1](https://arxiv.org/abs/1610.01644). More recently, Burns et al. demonstrated that latent "truth" directions can be recovered without labels using contrast-consistent search [2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.03827), while Park et al. formalized the *linear representation hypothesis* and characterized the geometry of concept subspaces in large language models [3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.03658). Sparse autoencoders have been proposed as a scalable way to discover monosemantic feature directions in the residual stream [4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.08600). COSMIC builds directly on this linear-concept view: rather than probing for interpretability, it selects and validates concept directions using cosine similarity in activation space.

**Activation steering and representation engineering.** A growing body of work manipulates model behavior by directly editing hidden activations. Inference-Time Intervention (ITI) shifts attention-head activations along truthfulness directions computed from labeled contrast pairs [5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.03341). ActAdd (Activation Addition) constructs steering vectors from a single pair of contrasting prompts and adds them at inference time [6](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.10248), while Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA) averages differences over many behavioral contrast pairs to steer Llama-2 on behaviors such as sycophancy and refusal [7](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.06681). Representation Engineering (RepE) provides a broader top-down framework for reading and controlling concepts including honesty, morality, and power-seeking [8](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.01405). In-Context Vectors likewise distill task demonstrations into latent-space steering signals [9](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.06668), and recent work aims for universal steering and monitoring across models and behaviors [10](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.03708). Most of these techniques rely on labeled contrast pairs and select layers or directions by measuring downstream output changes; COSMIC removes the need for this output-based supervision by using purely internal cosine-similarity criteria.

**Refusal directions and safety-relevant behaviors.** Closest to our work, Arditi et al. showed that refusal in a wide range of aligned chat models is mediated by a single one-dimensional subspace, and that erasing this direction disables refusal while adding it induces over-refusal [11](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.11717). Their pipeline, however, presupposes access to detectable refusal templates in the output and selects layers by evaluating refusal string frequencies. Trojan Activation Attacks exploit similar steering mechanics for red-teaming safety-aligned models [12](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09433). COSMIC generalizes this line of work by identifying viable refusal directions and target layers without any assumption about refusal-token patterns, enabling reliable steering even for weakly aligned or adversarially perturbed models.

**Jailbreaks and adversarial alignment.** Understanding refusal directions is tightly connected to jailbreak research. Wei et al. analyzed how safety training fails through competing objectives and mismatched generalization [13](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.02483). Zou et al. introduced the GCG suffix attack, an optimization-based universal jailbreak transferable across aligned LLMs [14](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.15043). Follow-up work shows that even leading safety-aligned models fall to simple adaptive attacks [15](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.02151) and to persuasive natural-language prompts [16](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.06373). In-context demonstrations have also been used both to jailbreak and to guard aligned models [17](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.06387). These attacks motivate COSMIC's evaluation in adversarial settings where output-based direction selection is unreliable because refusals are suppressed or disguised.

**Benchmarks for steering.** Recent benchmarks such as AxBench systematically compare steering methods and show that simple activation-based baselines can be highly competitive with more complex approaches [18](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.17148). COSMIC contributes to this line by providing an automated, output-independent direction-selection procedure that matches prior steering performance while broadening applicability to models whose refusal behavior cannot be characterized a priori.
