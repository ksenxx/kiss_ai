# Related Works

## Speech-Driven 3D Facial Animation

Data-driven 3D speech-driven facial animation has progressed rapidly since the release of paired audio–4D-scan corpora such as VOCASET, which was introduced together with VOCA, a subject-conditioned convolutional model that regresses per-vertex offsets on a template mesh from DeepSpeech features [1](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.03079). MeshTalk extended this line of work with a categorical latent space that explicitly disentangles audio-correlated (lip) motion from audio-uncorrelated upper-face motion, mitigating the tendency of regressed vertex offsets to collapse to a mean face [2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08223). FaceFormer replaced short convolutional receptive fields with a Transformer-based autoregressive decoder and, crucially, adopted a pre-trained wav2vec 2.0 encoder to inject phonetic context that is otherwise unrecoverable from clip-level acoustic features [3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.05329). CodeTalker recast the problem as a code-query task over a VQ-VAE motion codebook, arguing that a finite proxy space reduces cross-modal mapping uncertainty and alleviates over-smoothed lips [4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.02379). Imitator further personalized these audio-to-motion mappings so that identity-specific articulation styles are preserved [5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.00023), while ScanTalk generalized animation to unregistered raw scans, removing the reliance on topologically consistent template meshes [6](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.10942). Beyond monadic speaking, Learning to Listen investigated non-deterministic dyadic facial motion prediction, highlighting the multi-modal nature of audio-to-face mapping [7](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.08451). Our work is orthogonal to these architectural advances: Wav2Sem is a plug-and-play semantic decorrelation module that can be inserted in front of the audio encoder used by any of the above pipelines.

## Diffusion and Multi-Modal Facial Animation

More recently, diffusion models have been adapted to speech-driven 3D face generation. FaceDiffuser trains a denoising network conditioned on HuBERT-style speech embeddings, achieving richer facial motion than deterministic regressors [8](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.11306). DiffSpeaker combines diffusion with a biased-attention Transformer to model long-range temporal dependencies while retaining lip-sync accuracy [9](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.05712). DiffPoseTalk jointly models expressive facial motion and head pose in a stylistic diffusion framework [10](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.00434), and Media2Face extends generation to co-speech animation controlled by multi-modal (text, image, video) guidance signals atop a large-scale motion latent diffusion prior [11](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15687). Despite these architectural changes, virtually all of these systems still consume features from frozen self-supervised speech encoders and therefore inherit the same phoneme-averaging bottleneck that Wav2Sem targets.

## Self-Supervised Audio Representations

The prevalent choice of encoder in speech-driven animation is a self-supervised speech model. wav2vec introduced contrastive pre-training of raw-waveform convolutional features for downstream ASR [12](https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.05862), and wav2vec 2.0 generalized it to a masked-prediction Transformer with quantized latent targets [13](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11477). HuBERT provides an alternative masked-prediction objective over offline-clustered discrete units and is the backbone used in FaceXHuBERT for text-less expressive 3D face animation [14](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.07447)[15](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.05416). Whisper offers large-scale weakly-supervised representations that are increasingly used as speech encoders in animation pipelines [16](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.04356). While these representations excel at phoneme discrimination for ASR, near-homophonic syllables that share acoustic cues but differ in viseme shape (e.g., bilabial vs. labiodental onsets) remain tightly coupled in their feature spaces, an artifact that has been observed to cause averaged, indistinct lip motion in downstream animators [3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.05329)[4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.02379).

## Disentanglement and Semantic Guidance

Several works have attempted to disentangle latent factors in talking-face generation. EmoTalk explicitly separates emotional content from articulatory content to enable emotion-controllable animation [17](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.11089), and SelfTalk imposes a self-supervised commutative diagram that couples audio, mesh, and lip-reading text so that the learned motion latent must be linguistically decodable [18](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.10799). These approaches disentangle *outputs* (emotion, identity, semantics) rather than the *audio feature space itself*. In contrast, Wav2Sem introduces sequence-level semantic information to decorrelate near-homophonic audio encodings directly, complementing the above disentanglement objectives and consistently improving lip-motion precision across multiple state-of-the-art speech-driven backbones.
