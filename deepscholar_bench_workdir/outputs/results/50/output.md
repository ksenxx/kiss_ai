# Related Work

## Knowledge Graphs for Open-Source and ML Resource Management

Knowledge graphs (KGs) have been widely used to structure heterogeneous open-source artifacts so that they can be searched, linked, and recommended.  Early efforts in this direction target academic and code repositories: paper2repo [1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.06059) links research papers to their GitHub implementations through a distant-supervised recommender, but its schema covers only a narrow set of entity types and cannot express the fine-grained interdependencies observed on modern hubs such as Hugging Face.  In contrast, HuggingKG explicitly models model–model relations (adapter, finetune, merge, quantize) together with user interaction signals (publish, like, follow), yielding a KG that is both larger and semantically richer than prior ML-resource graphs.  Broader dataset and model observability efforts, such as the large-scale dataset licensing and attribution audit of the Data Provenance Initiative [2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.16787) and the community library that curates NLP datasets for the Hugging Face Hub [3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.02846), reinforce the need for structured, queryable representations of open-source ML assets — a gap our work directly addresses.  HuggingGPT [4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17580) further motivates such representations by demonstrating that a language-model controller can orchestrate hundreds of Hugging Face models when given structured metadata, showing why higher-quality relational data over the hub is valuable.

## Knowledge-Graph and Academic Benchmarks

A parallel line of work has produced benchmarks for graph-structured data.  The Open Graph Benchmark (OGB) [5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.00687) established standardized node-, link-, and graph-level tasks for large graphs, and OAG-Bench [6](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.15810) provides a human-curated benchmark for academic graph mining over the Open Academic Graph.  HuggingBench complements these resources by targeting a distinct domain — open-source ML resource management — and introducing three IR-oriented tasks (resource recommendation, task classification, and model tracing) that require reasoning over both structural relations and rich textual attributes of Hugging Face artifacts.  Library-level infrastructures such as CogDL [7](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.00959) further help operationalize such benchmarks with reproducible graph-learning pipelines.

## Knowledge Graph Embeddings

Our benchmark exercises a broad spectrum of KG embedding methods.  Tensor-factorization approaches such as DistMult [8](https://arxiv.org/abs/1412.6575) and ComplEx [9](https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.06357) model entities and relations as low-rank interactions, while RotatE [10](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.10197) formulates each relation as a rotation in complex space to capture symmetry, inversion, and composition patterns.  Convolutional and transformer variants such as ConvE [11](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.01476) and HittER [12](https://arxiv.org/abs/2008.12813) score triples with parameterized deep encoders.  More recently, ULTRA [13](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.04562) proposes a foundation-model-style approach that transfers across KGs with unseen entities and relations, which is particularly attractive for a growing, community-driven graph like HuggingKG.

## Graph Neural Networks and Node Classification

For the classification task, HuggingBench benchmarks the dominant families of graph neural networks.  GCN [14](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.02907) established spectral–spatial message passing for semi-supervised node classification, GraphSAGE [15](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.02216) introduced inductive neighbor sampling, and GAT [16](https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.10903) introduced attention-weighted aggregation.  Deeper and better-propagating variants such as APPNP [17](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.05997), GRAND [18](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.11079), and GCNII [19](https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.02133) address oversmoothing and enable training of substantially deeper models, all of which are relevant when propagating information over HuggingKG's millions of nodes and heterogeneous edge types.

## Recommendation over Interaction and Knowledge Graphs

The resource-recommendation task in HuggingBench is closely related to graph-based collaborative filtering and knowledge-aware recommendation.  LightGCN [20](https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.02126) simplifies GCN for collaborative filtering and remains a strong baseline; LightGCL [21](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.08191), SimGCL [22](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.08679), HCCF [23](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.12200), MHCN [24](https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.06448), AutoCF [25](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.07797), DCCF [26](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.02759), and the unified SSLRec framework [27](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.05697) explore self-supervised, contrastive, and hypergraph learning objectives.  Knowledge-aware recommenders such as KGIN [28](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.07057), KGCL [29](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.00976), and KGRec [30](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.02759) explicitly inject KG structure into user–item modeling, and RLMRec [31](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.15950) integrates large language models for representation-augmented recommendation.  These methods motivate HuggingBench's recommendation split, which — unlike prior developer-oriented repository recommenders such as paper2repo [1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.06059) — benefits from the dense inter-model and user-interaction relations captured by HuggingKG.

Together, these threads show that while both KG construction and KG-based IR methods are mature, existing resources do not cover the Hugging Face ecosystem at scale.  HuggingKG and HuggingBench aim to fill this gap by providing a large, richly typed graph and a multi-task IR benchmark tailored to open-source ML resource sharing and management.
