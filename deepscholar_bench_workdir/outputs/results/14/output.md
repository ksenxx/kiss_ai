# Related Works

## Real-World Image and Video Super-Resolution

Early real-world super-resolution research focused on convolutional restoration networks trained with elaborate synthetic degradation pipelines. Real-ESRGAN [1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.10833) introduced a second-order degradation model that greatly improved robustness on in-the-wild images. In the video domain, EDVR [2](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.02716) leveraged deformable convolutions for multi-frame alignment, while BasicVSR++ [3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.13371) refined recurrent propagation and alignment to establish a strong baseline for temporal reasoning. RealBasicVSR [4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.12704) extended these ideas to real-world settings by studying the trade-offs between input pre-cleaning and output quality. These regression-based methods often produce temporally stable but over-smoothed outputs, motivating the shift toward generative priors that our work also embraces.

## Diffusion Priors for Super-Resolution

Denoising diffusion probabilistic models [5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239) and their latent-space extension [6](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.10752) have redefined generative image restoration. StableSR [7](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.07015), DiffBIR [8](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.15070), PASD [9](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.14469) and SeeSR [10](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.16518) all repurpose Stable Diffusion for image restoration, injecting low-resolution conditions through time-aware encoders, ControlNet-like adapters [11](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.05543), pixel-aware modules, or semantic prompts. Complementary efforts such as DiffIR [12](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.09472), ResShift [13](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.12348) and SinSR [14](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.14760) accelerate sampling by learning compact priors or reformulating the diffusion process as residual shifting. While these methods deliver impressive perceptual quality, their stochasticity remains a source of hallucinated details and flickering artifacts—an issue that is amplified when transferred to video, and which our noise-robust framework explicitly targets.

## Video Diffusion and Diffusion-Based VSR

Extending diffusion models to the temporal axis, Video Diffusion Models [15](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.03458), Align-your-Latents [16](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.08818), AnimateDiff [17](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.04725) and Stable Video Diffusion [18](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.15127) equip 2D UNets with temporal attention or motion modules, providing pre-trained backbones for downstream video tasks. Building on such priors, Upscale-A-Video [19](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.06640) inserts temporal layers into Stable Diffusion and introduces latent propagation for smoother reconstructions, while MGLD-VSR [20](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.00853) exploits motion-guided latent diffusion to enforce inter-frame consistency in real-world VSR. However, temporal attention scales quadratically with sequence length, and both approaches still suffer from artifacts induced by the residual randomness of the diffusion prior, which our self-supervised ControlNet and three-stage training strategy are designed to mitigate.

## State Space Models for Vision

Mamba [21](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.00752) revived state-space models with an input-selective scan that attains linear-time global receptive fields. Vision Mamba [22](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.09417) and VMamba [23](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.10166) generalize this design to 2D imagery via bidirectional and cross-scan strategies, and VideoMamba [24](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.06977) further extends selective scans to spatio-temporal tokens for efficient video understanding. MambaIR [25](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.15648) and MambaIRv2 [26](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.15269) demonstrate that state-space blocks are competitive with transformers for image restoration at much lower cost. Motivated by these results, we adopt a 3D Selective Scan inside a Video State-Space block to obtain global spatio-temporal attention for VSR without incurring the prohibitive cost of full 3D self-attention.

## Self-Supervised and Contrastive Representation Learning

Self-supervised representation learning provides a natural way to obtain degradation-insensitive features. Contrastive frameworks such as SimCLR [27](https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.05709) and MoCo [28](https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.05722) learn embeddings invariant to strong augmentations by pulling positive pairs together and pushing negatives apart. We repurpose this principle inside a ControlNet-style branch: by contrasting HR- and LR-derived features, our self-supervised ControlNet extracts guidance that is robust to unknown real-world degradations, complementing the temporal modeling capacity of the Mamba-augmented diffusion backbone.
